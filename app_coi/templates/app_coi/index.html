  • New Paper: Ethnic Diversity Deflates Price Bubbles
    A new paper by David Stark and Sheen Levine finds that price bubbles are less likely to develop in markets comprised of ethnically diverse traders. Ethnic homogeneity promotes conformity and hence mispricing, while diversity leads to greater scrutiny and improved information processing.

  • ISERP Faculty Fellow Thomas DiPrete Examines "The Rise of Women"
    The Rise of Women: The Growing Gender Gap in Education and What it Means for American Schools, by ISERP Faculty Fellow and Giddings Professor of Sociology Thomas DiPrete (Columbia University) and Claudia Buchmann (Ohio State) presents their findings on the gender gap reversal in education, examining in detail why women now outperform men at all levels of school, and suggesting strategies to improve educational outcomes for both genders. Their research indicates that “the most important predictor of boys’ achievement is the extent to which the school culture expects, values, and rewards academic effort."
